Fit-out snagging for commercial projects

How to inspect a commercial fit-out, record defects by area and trade, and produce a report that keeps the job on programme.

See how it works

A commercial fit-out compresses a lot of trades into a short programme, and the snagging at the end is usually done under time pressure with the client already planning to move in. The risk is a rushed list that misses items, or one so disorganised that nobody can act on it before the deadline.

Inspect by area, then by trade

Fit-outs are easiest to snag area by area: reception, open plan, meeting rooms, kitchen, WCs, plant. Within each area, work through the trades in a consistent order so the same categories get checked every time. That gives a list that can be split and sent to the right contractor without rewriting it.

What to look for

  • Finishes: paint coverage, wall protection, floor joins and transitions, ceiling tile alignment.
  • Joinery: reception desks, kitchen units, doors and ironmongery, alignment and damage.
  • Services: sockets and data points, lighting, sensors, grilles and diffusers sitting flush.
  • Glazing and partitions: seals, alignment, manifestation, door closers.
  • Cleanliness and protection: marks from the works, protection left in place or removed.

Record defects so they can be split

Because a fit-out involves many contractors, a defect needs enough detail for the right trade to own it. A photograph, a specific location and a plain description are the minimum. SnagDesk tags each snag to a location so the report can be handed to a trade for their area without editing.

Report while you are still on site

On a tight programme, the value of a report drops by the hour. Generating it on your phone before you leave means the trades can start closing items the same day rather than waiting for a write-up.

Common questions

How is fit-out snagging different?

A fit-out involves many trades on a short programme, so defects need to be recorded by area and detailed enough to be split between contractors.

What should I inspect in a fit-out?

Work area by area through finishes, joinery, services, glazing and partitions, and check protection and cleanliness from the works.

How quickly should the report go out?

Same day where possible. On a tight fit-out programme a report that arrives days later has lost most of its value.
