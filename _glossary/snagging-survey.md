The term is most often used by buyers of new build homes, who commission an independent inspector to go through the property before or shortly after legal completion and produce a report they can send to the developer.

## What a snagging survey covers

A methodical walk through every room and the exterior, checking the same categories in each: walls and ceilings, floors, doors and windows, sockets and switches, fitted units, sanitaryware, and external finishes. The output is a numbered list of defects, each photographed and located.

## Who carries one out

Either the buyer themselves, or a professional snagging inspector. There is no single mandatory qualification for snagging inspection in the UK, so if you are hiring someone it is worth asking what their report actually looks like and how many items a typical survey records.

A homeowner doing it personally can produce a perfectly effective list. The advantages of a professional are experience of what commonly goes wrong on new builds and a formal, branded document that developers are used to receiving.

## What happens next

The report goes to the developer, who works through the items during the defects period. The strength of your position depends almost entirely on how specific the report is: photographed, located and dated items are difficult to dismiss, and vague ones are easy to.
