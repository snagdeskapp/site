Short for operation and maintenance manual, and sometimes issued as part of a wider building manual or health and safety file. On larger projects it is a substantial set of documents; on a domestic build it may be a folder of appliance instructions and certificates.

## What it typically contains

- **As-installed information** for services such as heating, ventilation, electrical and water systems
- **Manufacturer literature** and warranties for installed equipment
- **Maintenance schedules**, including what needs servicing and how often
- **Test and commissioning records**
- **Certificates**, for example electrical installation and gas safety
- **Contact details** for the installers and suppliers

## Why it matters at handover

The client cannot properly operate or maintain the building without it, and on many contracts the O&M documentation is a condition of practical completion. Missing or incomplete O&M information is itself frequently recorded as an outstanding item at [handover](/glossary/handover/).

It is worth treating it the same way as a snag list: something to be checked off explicitly rather than assumed to have arrived.
