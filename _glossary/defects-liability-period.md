Also called the defects rectification period or, on some contracts, the rectification period. Its length is set by the contract, and a period of six or twelve months from practical completion is common on UK projects, though it varies.

## What it covers

It covers defects in the works that appear or are identified during the period, typically faults in workmanship or materials rather than damage caused by the occupier or ordinary wear. Items on the snag list produced at practical completion are usually closed out during this window.

## What it is not

It is not a warranty on everything forever, and it is not a maintenance agreement. It also does not usually cover:

- Damage caused by how the building is used after handover
- Normal wear and tear
- Alterations made by the occupier

## Why recording matters

Because the period is time-limited, evidence and dates matter. A defect photographed, located and reported within the period is straightforward. The same defect raised verbally, months later, with no record of when it was first seen, is where disputes come from.

Keeping a dated, photographed record from handover onwards is the simplest protection for both sides. That is one reason a [site record](/use-cases/site-diary/) is worth keeping even after the job is finished.
