Builders don't have time to stop, sit down and write things up. Snags get spotted mid-task, on a ladder or halfway through a second fix, and if there's no quick way to record them they get forgotten until the client finds them first. SnagDesk is built to catch a defect in the ten seconds you have, so nothing slips and the list is accurate when it matters.

A day with SnagDesk

You're finishing the first fix on a two-storey build. In the back bedroom you notice a joint that needs redoing. Instead of trying to remember it, you open SnagDesk, photograph it, drop a circle on the joint, tag it "Bedroom 2" and add "re-do plasterboard joint". Ten seconds, done, and you're back on the tools.

You carry on like that through the day, room by room. There's no connection on this plot, and it doesn't matter, because everything saves on the phone. At the end of the week you generate a report for the site manager, with every snag located and photographed, and it takes one tap rather than an evening at a laptop.

Built for how builders work

Ten-second capture

Photograph, mark up and note a snag fast enough to keep working.

Room by room

Tag each snag to its location so nothing lands against the wrong room.

No signal needed

Works on any plot, connection or not. It all stays on your phone.

An accurate list, not a guess

When snags are caught as they happen, the list is complete and honest. That means fewer nasty surprises at handover, less rework chasing things you half-remembered, and a cleaner relationship with the site manager and the client. The report at the end is just a tap away.
